Current list

Games that stand out for different reasons.

Some are here for world-building, some for system depth, and some because the hardware setup sounds almost as interesting as the game itself.

Adventure

The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom

Open-world experimentation, creative systems, and the kind of design that keeps rewarding curiosity.

RPG

Baldur's Gate 3

Deep role-playing systems, party dynamics, and a scale that makes it feel worth settling into for a while.

Racing

Forza Horizon 5

The big question is not just the game itself, but whether it deserves a steering wheel setup to really feel right.

Strategy

Age of Wonders 4

Looks compelling, but also feels like the kind of game that asks for a real time commitment once it starts clicking.
